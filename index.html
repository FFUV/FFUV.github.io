About Oppenheimer

J. Robert Oppenheimer was an American theoretical physicist and the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, the World War II project that developed the first nuclear weapons. Known as the "father of the atomic bomb," Oppenheimer's work changed the course of history.

His famous quote, "I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds," reflects the profound impact of his contributions to science and the ethical questions surrounding nuclear technology.
